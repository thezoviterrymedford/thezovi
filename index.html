How Zovi actually works

Two feeds. One simple idea.

It starts just like the apps you already use. Then it becomes something they can't do.

1

Your Personal Feed

Just like Instagram or TikTok

This part feels familiar. Like, save, and import content from TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube — exactly like you already do. Or post your own — photos, videos, a quick tweet-style thought, whatever's on your mind. Keep it private and it just builds your taste profile, or go public and let people follow you. Either way, it's the foundation everything else is built on.

↓   This is where Zovi stops looking like every other app   ↓
2

Your Group Feed

Nothing else does this

Create or join a group with your real friends — your crew, your family, your golf buddies, whoever. Every night, Zovi combines everyone's personal feed in that group into one shared feed. Your golf-obsessed friend's saves show up next to your sister's recipe finds — all in one feed, automatically, with zero effort from anyone. You never had to send a single link.

A personal feed alone is just another app. A group feed built from the people you actually trust is something else entirely — a wider lens into what your crew loves, without anyone lifting a finger.
